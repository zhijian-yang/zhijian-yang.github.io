About

I am currently an AI Scientist at GE Healthcare, where I focus on developing medical foundation models.

I completed my Ph.D. in Applied Math and Computational Science at the University of Pennsylvania under the guidance of Christos Davatzikos. My PhD research focused on AI in healthcare, with specific focuses on generative models, representation learning, and multi-modal learning. During my Ph.D., I primarily developed deep learning models aimed at disentangling disease heterogeneity from three distinct methodological perspectives:

  • Patient clustering based on disease-related imaging patterns
  • Interpretable representation learning of disease-related imaging patterns
  • Multi-modal disease subtyping with imaging and genetic signatures

    • For future research, I have a broad interest in machine learning and deep learning, particularly their applications to healthcare and medical-related problems.
